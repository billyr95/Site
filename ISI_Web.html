Process

Highlighting the process of how I build and design pages

  • Step 1

    Identify

    Identify which pages are underperforming on the site. Figure out whether it's SEO, design, or speed. From there I decide how to approach the reworking of the page.

  • Step 2

    Lo-Fi Mockup

    After I identify some issues, I begin mocking up a lo-fi wire frame, this helps me figure out placement of content, padding, margin, and for our writers what a good work count can be for content.

  • Step 3

    Hi-Fi Mockup

    This step is the most important to me, I design the page to look exactly as it will when it's live. The colors, imagery, the visual hierarchy. All of this I do prior to coding at all to save time and receive approval.

  • Step 4

    Development

    From here I code the page out to the exact specification of the mock up, I stay mostly true to it, but sometimes things change. I start by structuring out all of the HTML and placing content, and palceholders for anything that involves JavaScript. After the HTML I do all the CSS minus the media queries. Once the main CSS is complete I do all my JavaScript coding and finally do all of my responsive work.

  • Step 5

    Launch

    Everything from forms, JS, and responsiveness is tested prior to launch to ensure the best possible experience I can deliver. When launching we use Google Optimize to AB test the page, and stay vigilant should any issues come up. /p>

  • Step 6

    Post-Launch

    After the AB test is run I look at the results and figure out how successful the page was, regardless of any short comings what try and figure out what works and what didn't for future.
