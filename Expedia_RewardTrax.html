Expedia RewardTrax Demo and Website

What is the Exepdia RewardTrax Demo and Website?

On this project we had the oppurtunity to land one of our biggest cliients ever Expedia. I was the assigned designer for building and designing the demo for the sales team and then once we won the contract I was also the assigned designer for their actual website.

In this case study, I will be sharing my process and rationale behind some of my decisions that I made designing for Expedia and to explore the process I took on working on such a large impactful project.

The Demo

After we got far enough down the Sale's funnel the sales team reached out to me to build a functioning demo for Exepdia. When we do a demo it's essentially a striped down version of how an actual client site would work and look like.

Post Sale

After we sold the program to Expedia, the Account Manager got me and our software dev team on the same page, for this program there wasn't a lot of back end custom work.

Goals

Here are some of the goals we had established for this program, due to how important they are as a client:

  • Faster turnaround, we usually launch in 8 weeks, for this we had a 6 week timeline
  • Less client edits
  • One more goal

Process:

I was brought ontoo design both the demo and final site. Here I will outline my process for working on this client's site

Branding

The first thing I did was get more familiar with Expedia's branding, so I could get a better understanding of how I will use their colors and typeface accross the site.

Client's input

I spoke with the account manager and client on a call discussing overall look and feel they wanted, asked questions regarding branding and how they'd want the images displayed across the site.
